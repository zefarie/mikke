# Kinematics — lecture notes, week 2

One-dimensional motion with constant acceleration. The three equations to
memorize: v = v0 + at ; x = x0 + v0·t + ½at² ; v² = v0² + 2a(x − x0).

Free fall is the classic special case: a = −g with g ≈ 9.81 m/s², air
resistance neglected. A dropped object and a horizontally thrown object hit
the ground at the same time — vertical and horizontal motions are
independent.

Worked example in class: a ball thrown upward at 15 m/s reaches its highest
point after about 1.5 s, at roughly 11.5 m.
